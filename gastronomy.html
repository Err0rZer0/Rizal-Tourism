The Flavors of Rizal

From the sweet, sticky perfection of Antipolo's suman to the adventurous exotic dishes of Angono, Rizal offers a culinary journey that perfectly blends traditional Filipino flavors with homegrown creativity.

Suman sa Ibos

Antipolo's famous sticky rice delicacy wrapped in palm leaves, best paired with sweet ripe mangoes or dipped in raw sugar.

Roasted Kasoy

Freshly roasted cashew nuts sourced from the hills of Antipolo, offering a rich, buttery crunch that serves as the perfect pasalubong.

Minaluto

A feast of mixed seafood, meat, vegetables, and rice served in a traditional bilao, popularized by the famous Balaw-Balaw restaurant in Angono.

Fried Itik

Angono and Taytay's signature deep-fried native duck, marinated in a special blend of spices until crispy on the outside and tender on the inside.

Cainta's Kakanin

Explore the "Bibingka Capital" and indulge in an assortment of sweet native rice cakes, including sapin-sapin, biko, and latik-topped maja blanca.

Mountain Coffee

Sip locally sourced, freshly brewed coffee from the many overlooking cafes in Tanay and Antipolo, accompanied by the cold Sierra Madre breeze.
