A Landscape of Contrasts

From the urban edges bordering Metro Manila to the sprawling, rugged peaks of the Sierra Madre, Rizal’s geography is a spectacular mosaic.

The Sierra Madre Range

Dominating the eastern half of the province, this massive mountain range provides lush forests, dramatic limestone cliffs, and the famous sea of clouds in Tanay and Rodriguez.

Laguna de Bay

Bordering the southern municipalities, the largest lake in the Philippines provides a vital source of livelihood and scenic lakeside sunsets.

The Valley Fault

The western municipalities (like Cainta and Taytay) sit in the Marikina Valley, serving as the bustling, urbanized gateway to Metro Manila.

The Wawa Gorge

A stunning geographical fault in Rodriguez where the Marikina River cuts through two towering limestone mountains, Mt. Pamitinan and Mt. Binacayan.

Karst Formations

The province is famous for its karst landscapes, creating hidden cave systems and the pristine, white-marble boulders of the Tinipak River.
