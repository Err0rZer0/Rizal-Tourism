The Art Capital’s Masterpieces

Journey through Angono and beyond to witness the soul of Filipino creativity, from historic petroglyphs to world-class contemporary galleries.

Pinto Art Museum

A Mediterranean-inspired museum in Antipolo housing a vast collection of modern Filipino masterpieces across several open-air villas.

Angono Petroglyphs

The oldest known work of art in the Philippines, featuring 127 human and animal figures engraved on a rock wall dating back to 3000 BC.

Blanco Family Museum

Located in Angono, this museum showcases the collective talent of the Blanco family, known for their realistic depictions of Filipino life.

Nemiranda Arthouse

Step into the creative world of Nemi Miranda, the "Father of Imaginative Figurism," featuring stunning sculptures and mystical paintings.

Tiamson Art Gallery

A vibrant gallery in Angono dedicated to the eccentric and multi-talented artist, musician, and true Rizal local, Orville Tiamson.

Botong Francisco Studio

The preserved workspace and ancestral home of National Artist Carlos "Botong" Francisco, celebrating his iconic historical murals.
