Travel Requirements & Guidelines

Everything you need to know about environmental fees, tour guide policies, and entry protocols before exploring Rizal's natural wonders.

Local Environmental Fees

Most hiking trails, waterfalls, and eco-parks in Rizal (especially in Tanay and Rodriguez) require a small environmental fee (usually ₱50 to ₱100) payable at the barangay hall.

Mandatory Tour Guides

To protect the environment and ensure safety, hiking major peaks (like Mt. Daraitan or Mt. Pamitinan) mandates hiring a registered local guide. Ratios are strictly enforced.

Reservations & Walk-ins

While many sites accept walk-ins, premium spots like Masungi Georeserve or upscale dining like Tahanan Bistro strictly require advanced online reservations.

The Hiker's Checklist

  • Valid ID (Often left at the barangay jump-off point for safety tracking)
  • Cash in small denominations (Many remote areas have no ATMs or GCash signal)
  • Eco-friendly water bottle (Single-use plastics are banned on most trails)
  • A printed or digital copy of your confirmed reservation (if applicable)
