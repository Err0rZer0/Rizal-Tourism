Echoes of the Past

Walk through the history of the Philippine Revolution and ancient heritage sites that shaped the identity of the province.

Wawa Dam

A gravity dam built during the American era, legendary as the site where Bernardo Carpio supposedly fought for freedom.

Pamitinan Cave

The historic site where Andres Bonifacio and the Katipuneros first declared independence from Spain in 1895.

Boso-Boso Ruins

The remains of a 16th-century church that survived wars and earthquakes, standing as a testament to Rizal's resilience.

Calinawan Cave

Served as a secret shelter for Filipino revolutionaries against Spanish colonizers and a hideout during WWII.

Angono Petroglyphs

A National Cultural Treasure showcasing the oldest known artwork in the country dating back to the late Neolithic period.

Manansala Shrine

The historical home of National Artist Vicente Manansala in Binangonan, preserving his cubist masterpieces and legacy.
